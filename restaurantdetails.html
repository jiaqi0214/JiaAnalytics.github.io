Overview

This restaurant database project showcases SQL queries used to analyze sales performance, menu insights, and customer ordering trends. The dataset consists of two key tables: order_details and menu_items, which are joined to extract meaningful business insights. Queries include identifying the most and least ordered menu items, calculating average dish prices by category, and determining the top revenue-generating orders. Additionally, the project explores menu composition by counting dishes in each category and analyzing price distributions. By leveraging SQL for data-driven decision-making, this project highlights how restaurants can optimize their menu offerings and improve customer experiences.

1. Sales Performance

  • Top Revenue-Generating Items: Identified using joined data to prioritize high-income menu items.
  • Least Ordered Items: Highlighted underperforming dishes for potential removal or promotion.
  • Order Volume Trends: Aggregated counts provided insights into customer favorites.

2. Pricing Insights

  • Average Price by Category: Helped assess consistency and guide competitive pricing.
  • Price Distribution Analysis: Showed pricing diversity and pinpointed outliers across categories.

3. Menu Composition

  • Dish Count per Category: Evaluated menu balance across different types of dishes.
  • Category Popularity: Determined sales contribution of each menu section.

4. Customer Ordering Behavior

  • Most Common Orders: Revealed frequently ordered items and popular combinations.
  • Revenue by Order: Analyzed total spend per order to identify high-value transactions.

5. Operational Recommendations

  • Menu Optimization: Focus on best-sellers and refine or remove less popular items.
  • Pricing Strategy: Use insights to adjust prices based on category averages and margins.
  • Customer Experience: Leverage order trends to enhance service and suggest popular pairings.
